The aggregation of peptides into β-sheet-rich amyloid fibrils has been implicated in several age-related diseases such as Alzheimer's or type 2 diabetes. The influence of nanoparticles and biomembranes on peptide oligomer and fibril formation was studied to understand the role of surfaces on aggregation kinetics. In addition, the relationship between antimicrobial peptides and amyloid formation was investigated.
